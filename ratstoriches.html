Process Insights

Rats to Riches is a work-in-progress capstone project where I serve as one of the programmers. The game places players in the role of a wizard who, after experimenting with dangerous spells, accidentally transforms into a rat. To reverse the spell, the player must journey through a dungeon and collect enough gems to regain the mana required to return to human form.

The development of Rats to Riches went through several major pivots before settling into its current form. Our first concept was a city-based rail-grinding auto-scroller, combining fast-paced traversal with the tension of constant forward momentum. While it was an exciting premise, the scope of creating a full city environment proved too large for our capstone timeframe. To adapt, we shifted the game into a procedurally generated dungeon crawler. This allowed us to reuse spaces creatively, experiment with replayability, and focus our efforts on smaller but more impactful design elements.

As development continued, we realised that the auto-scrolling mechanic from the first concept still had potential to elevate the experience. By merging the dungeon crawler design with the auto-scrolling element, the game took on a unique identity as a procedurally generated auto-scroller dungeon crawler. This hybrid approach gave Rats to Riches a strong gameplay hook—constant pressure from the scrolling environment combined with the tension of navigating traps, enemies, and procedural layouts.

From a programming perspective, one of my biggest challenges was implementing enemy AI and trap systems. Getting enemies to behave in a way that was both functional and engaging within a constantly moving, procedurally generated environment required significant iteration. Pathfinding became especially tricky since the auto-scrolling mechanics meant the level was never static. Similarly, trap placement and activation needed careful tuning to feel fair while still providing a consistent challenge to the player. These systems required multiple redesigns before they felt reliable in the context of our hybrid design.

Overall, Rats to Riches has been a valuable exercise in flexibility and problem-solving. Each pivot, from the city auto-scroller to the dungeon crawler to the final hybrid design, reflects our team’s ability to adapt concepts to the project’s scope and strengths. For me personally, tackling the AI and trap challenges deepened my understanding of systemic design within procedural environments and reinforced the importance of iteration when working with complex mechanics.
